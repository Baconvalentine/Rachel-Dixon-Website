Struggling with weight gain, sugar cravings, brain fog, or poor sleep?

If you’re peri- or post-menopause, your body may be changing in ways that feel confusing, frustrating, and exhausting.

You’ve probably tried to manage on your own. Maybe even tried medication. And when it didn’t help, you just kept going.

You don’t have to keep powering through.

Does this sound familiar?

You might be experiencing:

  • Heavy or painful periods
  • Hot flushes or night sweats
  • Broken or unrefreshing sleep
  • Brain fog or poor concentration
  • Mood swings or feeling emotionally flat
  • Energy crashes and strong sugar cravings
  • Weight gain despite eating “well”

    • You’re busy, capable, and used to coping — but what used to work for your body no longer does. And that can feel unsettling.

    There is another way to support your health

    Many symptoms during peri- and post-menopause are linked to stress, hormone changes, and how the body responds to constant pressure.

    This isn’t about willpower or “trying harder”.

    With the right understanding and support, you can work with your body — not against it — using small, achievable changes that fit into real life.

    This approach helps you:

  • Understand why your symptoms are happening
  • Reduce sugar cravings and energy slumps
  • Feel calmer and more balanced
  • Build habits you can rely on long-term

    • A holistic, real-life approach to menopause health

    I’m Rachel, a naturopathic health coach specialising in supporting women aged 40+.

    I take a relaxed, holistic approach that looks at the whole picture — stress, lifestyle, habits, and hormones — without rigid rules, strict diets, or overwhelm.

    My role is to guide and educate you, so you gain the tools and confidence to manage your own health going forward.

    This is supportive, practical, and designed around your life.

    I’ve been a nurse for over 20 years, but often felt there wasn’t the time or space within conventional healthcare to support all aspects of a person’s life.

    At the age of 51, I chose to focus on a more natural, holistic approach to health and wellbeing. I trained with the College of Naturopathic Medicine, gaining a Level 5 Diploma in Health Coaching, and I’m a member of the Association of Naturopathic Practitioners (ANP).

    I’m also on my own health journey — learning how to nourish my body, manage stress, and maintain energy at this stage of life. I truly understand these challenges, both professionally and personally.

    • How the programme works

    All sessions take place in the comfort of your own home and are designed to fit around a busy lifestyle.

    Initial Session – 60 minutes

    We explore your health history, current symptoms, and what’s troubling you most. Together, we start to understand why you feel the way you do.

    Ongoing Support – 5 x 30-minute sessions

  • Five fortnightly sessions over 10 weeks, giving you time to make changes, reflect, and build momentum.
  • We focus on achievable goals, review what’s working, and gently adjust what isn’t — without overwhelm.
  • This is about steady progress, not perfection.

    • Got a question?

    Is this right for peri- or post-menopause?

    Both. I work with women 40+ who are peri- or post-menopause and experiencing changes in energy, mood, weight, sleep, or hormones.

    I’m very busy — will this take a lot of time?

    No. Sessions are short, spaced out, and designed to fit around real life. There’s no overwhelm or pressure to do everything at once.

    Will you give me meal plans or supplements?

    No. I don’t prescribe meal plans or supplements. Instead, I help you understand how food, stress, and habits affect your energy and hormones so you can make informed choices.

    I don’t really know what’s wrong — is that okay?

    Absolutely. “I just don’t feel like myself” is a very common starting point. We’ll gently figure things out together.

    Is this a replacement for medical care?

    No. Health coaching complements, but does not replace, medical care. I don’t diagnose or treat medical conditions.
